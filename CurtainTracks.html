Our Available Options

Ordinary curtain tracks

Ordinary curtain tracks, also referred to as standard curtain tracks, are basic systems used to hang curtains or draperies. These tracks are typically made from metal or plastic and are available in various lengths and configurations to accommodate different window sizes and curtain styles.

Ordinary curtain tracks Brochure

Ripple Curtain Tracks

Ripple curtain tracks, also known as ripple fold tracks, are a type of curtain track system designed to create uniform and consistent waves or ripples in the curtain fabric when the curtains are drawn closed. This style of curtain track is often used in modern and contemporary interior design schemes due to its sleek and streamlined appearance. The key feature of ripple curtain tracks is the specially designed carriers or gliders that attach to the curtain fabric. These carriers are spaced evenly along the track and are typically inserted into small pockets sewn into the back of the curtain panels. As the curtains are drawn across the track, the carriers create gentle, uniform ripples or waves in the fabric.

Ripple curtain tracks Brochure

Bendable Curtain Tracks

Bendable curtain tracks, also known as flexible curtain tracks or curved curtain tracks, are designed to be easily shaped to fit the contours of non-standard or irregularly shaped windows, such as bay windows or arched windows. These tracks are made from materials that can be bent and flexed without breaking, allowing for customization to suit specific window shapes and angles.

Bendable Curtain Tracks Brochure

Corded Curtain Tracks

Corded curtain tracks are a type of curtain track system that features a cord or pulley mechanism, allowing you to easily open and close your curtains with minimal effort. These tracks are typically used for heavier curtains or when a more effortless, controlled movement is desired.

Curtain Rods

Curtain rods are essential home decor elements that help hang and support curtains or drapes. They come in a variety of materials, styles, and sizes to match different window treatments and room aesthetics

Standard Rods: Simple rods that hold curtains in place, suitable for lightweight to medium-weight fabric.

Decorative Rods: These are designed to be an attractive addition to the room. They come in various finishes (wood, metal, etc.) and may have decorative finials (end caps) for added style.

Traverse Rods: These have a mechanism that allows curtains to be opened and closed using a cord. Ideal for heavier or lined drapery.

Double Rods: Used for hanging a sheer curtain and a heavier curtain separately, adding depth and layering options to the window treatment.
